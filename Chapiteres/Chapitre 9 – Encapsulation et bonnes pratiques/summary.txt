



>>> the primaty goal of this chapter is learning how 
to protect your class data to ensure the system
always functions correctly.

- data encapsulation:

> use the private keyword for properties.
> this prevents direct access 
or accidental modification from outside the class.

-getters and setters:

> getter : provide read access to a private property.
> setter : provide write access to a private property.

- validation and sanitization in setters:

> setters ate the ideal place to chech data before it is stored.

> Examples :

_ strlen : to ensure minimum length.
_ htmlspecialchars : to prevent xss and ensure data sanitization.


conclusion :

encapsulation isn't just about security it's about building robust 
and reliable classes that protect their internal state.
